# Source: SBU Official Course Evaluation — ESE 224, Professor Alexa Doboli

## What was valuable about this course

Data structures themselves are very valuable to know, and the labs associated with the course are valuable in actually programming these data structures. Professor Doboli is very effective in teaching this subject, as well as the co-instructor Bilal El Jamal. Both were extremely knowledgeable on this subject and thoroughly explained everything. Bilal provides real-world insight into how these concepts and data structures are actually used in industry, given that he worked in the industry for many years. The combination of Professor Doboli's teaching experience with Bilal's industry experience made this class very enjoyable.

## What could be improved about this course

The professor spent too much time on singly/doubly/circular linked lists so we didn't really get a chance to learn the other data structures. The tests are questionable because you get a whole cheat sheet and have to write a whole program from scratch. It sort of defeats the purpose of programming if we just copy and paste data structures that we had coded in labs and lectures and write them on the test.

---

We spent a very long time on linked lists and its derivatives, and as a result we didn't get to spend as much time on more complicated data structures, such as the tree and BST. We did get to cover heaps and hash maps, but I wish there was a short lab for them for us to actually implement them.
